Visual system audit & language

An honest look at what's there, what's working, and the evolution I'd commit to. Read this before the surface tabs — every screen downstream is built on these choices.

Audit of the current system00.1

Pulling apart the existing tokens. Keep, evolve, kill.

Dark-first ✓ keep

The brand lives at night. Athletes are mid-effort, indoors under bad gym lighting, or outside at dawn. Dark-first is correct. Decision: commit harder — light mode is a "respect the OS" courtesy, not a co-equal design.

SF Pro throughout ✓ keep

It's iOS-native, ships free, and Apple Fitness uses it. Evolve: reserve SF Pro Rounded for timer / hero numerics only. SF Pro Text for everything else. The rounded everywhere is too soft for "earned confidence."

Coral #FF3B30 ⚠ evolve

It's iOS system red. Means "destructive" to half your users. Same hex is "stop / cancel / delete." Recommend shifting 8° hotter to #FF4530 — keeps the burning warmth, lets it read as focus not danger. Sub-12% of any surface.

Cards everywhere ⚠ evolve

Cards as the ONLY unit makes every screen feel like a settings page. Recommend reserving cards for: feed items, history items, race summary blocks. In-race screens are typographic, not cardified. Profile hero is full-bleed, not boxed.

12pt radius ⚠ evolve

12pt is mid-2010s iOS. Recommend a two-tier radius: 16pt for cards, 22pt for full-bleed sheets and pills. Anything pressable bigger than 56pt tall gets 22pt.

24pt margin ✓ keep

Apple Fitness standard. Don't fight it.

Success / warning greens ⚠ evolve

System greens are friendly. In race context they need to be tools, not encouragement. Recommend a colder #2BC758 for "ON PACE" so it doesn't compete with coral for emotional weight.

Type scale ⚠ thin it out

Race timer / hero / title / headline / body / caption / caps — that's 7 sizes. Real life: you'll use 4. Race-only 72pt, hero 28pt, body 17pt, caps 10pt. Cut display 26pt and title 22pt — they overlap.

Motion spring 0.4/0.8 ✓ keep

It's iOS-native and not bouncy. Lock it as the only spring in the system. Document three motion roles: state-change, alert-takeover, ambient (HR pulse). Nothing else gets to animate.

Motion principles00.5

Three motion roles. Anything that doesn't fit one of these doesn't get to animate.

1 · State change

spring(0.4, 0.8) · card mount, sheet present, tab swap. The default. 90% of motion.

2 · Alert takeover

200ms ease-out scale 0.9→1.0 + fade · the 5 watch overlays. Hard, decisive, no bounce. Paired with haptic.

3 · Ambient

1.4s ease-in-out pulse · live HR dot, active streak flame. Background life. Disabled under Reduce Motion.

Iconography & illustration stance00.6

Icons → SF Symbols only

Custom icons are a tax. Use SF Symbols at 17pt for nav, 22pt for headers, 28pt for in-race controls. Weight: medium. Filled variant only for active tab state.

Station glyphs

16 HYROX stations need real glyphs — these are content, not chrome. Commission a custom set: outlined, 2pt stroke, monoline, 64×64 art-board. This is the one place we hand-draw.

No illustrations

No empty-state characters. No mascots. Empty states are typographic with a single SF Symbol glyph at 56pt, 30% opacity. Strava-quiet.

No gradients

One exception: a single coral radial glow behind the "READY TO RACE" title and the predicted-finish PB callout. Otherwise flat.

Voice on copy00.7

Short. Honest. Observational. The product noticed something — it doesn't congratulate you for showing up.

Not Trakrr

The system in one paragraph00.8

Dark by default. SF Pro Rounded for the clock and nothing else. Coral only where a brand moment lives — a PB, a "ready to race," the primary button. Cards earn their place; race screens are typographic cathedrals. Motion is one spring, plus a hard takeover for alerts, plus an ambient pulse. Voice says what it noticed and stops.
