09 · Reference

Component library — what to steal, what to invent

The brief calls out Strava, Whoop, Apple Fitness, Linear, Things. You also asked me to look at Airbnb and Uber. Below: each Trakrr component, the best-in-class precedent for it, the lift, and what we change to make it ours. The right answer is rarely "invent" — most of these patterns are solved problems. Our job is to assemble them with taste.

Each block: what others do → what Trakrr does → why. Stealing the right idea is a feature, not a bug.

Why this works

Lifted: Waze's verb-first formula. One word that tells you what to do, not what's happening. "TURN RIGHT" not "intersection ahead." We do the same: HOLD, SLOW, REDLINE, RECOVER, PUSH.

Changed: Five color-states. Each gets a distinct haptic pattern. The takeover is full-screen on Watch (1.5-sec glance distance), a top-banner card on phone (arm's length, less invasive).

Killed: The instinct to add HR graphs to the overlay. You're 4 stations deep, you don't need a graph — you need a verb.

Why this works

Lifted: Apple TV's six-character code is the cleanest pair-code pattern in the wild. Grouped 3-3 lets you read it once and remember it.

Cross-app pattern brainstorm09.2

Things I'd consider stealing into Trakrr — not committed yet, just on the table.

Airbnb · "Wishlists" → Race targets

Airbnb's "save to a list with a name" pattern → save races as targets ("Sub-90," "Beat my London time"). They show up on profile as collections.

Uber · "Confirm with slide"

Slide-to-confirm gesture for the final race-end action so you don't accidentally end at station 14. Friction here is correct, not annoying.

Strava · Segment leaderboards

Apply to per-station times — fastest Wall Balls in your division this month. This is half the moat.

Whoop · Recovery ring

Don't copy the ring (theirs is iconic). Steal the idea: a single recovery number, 0–100, gating today's training rec. Lives on profile, top-right of Next Up card.

Apple Fitness · Sharing competition

"Compete this week with @sarahc" — 7-day duel scored by combined HYROX-relevant minutes. Lightweight, social, recurring.

Linear · ⌘K command bar

For power users: pull-down search on Profile = command palette. "Start race · custom workout · find athlete · last race." Optional, but rewards the 5%.

Things · Heading rhythm

Things' "Today / Upcoming / Anytime" headers as the rhythm for Profile: "Next Up / Recent / Trends." Reads like a journal, not a dashboard.

Notion · Inline reactions on comments

Race comment threads should support tap-to-react inline (not just on the parent race). Strava can't do this. We can.

Headspace · Single-color tinting

Each station gets a tint color from the HYROX palette. The tint follows the station through history, summary, station intelligence — gives 16 stations 16 personalities.

Robinhood · Big-number screen

The in-race timer screen IS Robinhood's portfolio view. One huge number, sparse context, no buttons fighting for attention. We've already half-built this — commit to it.

What NOT to steal09.3

Duolingo's everything

Streaks with cartoon characters, XP bars, mascot reactions. Antithesis of "earned confidence."

Instagram's stories tray

You're not a content platform. Don't put a horizontal scroll of avatars at the top of feed.

TikTok's algorithmic feed

Trakrr's feed is chronological, from people you follow. Don't optimize for engagement — optimize for trust.

Spotify's hyper-personalized cards

"Your Wrapped" is great once a year. Per-screen personalized greetings ("Crushing it, Umang!") are not.

The principle

Steal the assembly, not the aesthetic. Airbnb's hero, Uber's CTA discipline, Apple Fitness's tile rhythm, Linear's empty states, Waze's verbs, iMessage's gestures, Apple TV's pair codes — these are solved problems. We assemble them with HYROX context, our voice, and a coral-red focus accent. The Trakrr-ness is in the combination and restraint, not in any one custom-built shape.
